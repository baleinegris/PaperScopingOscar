## Abstract
Silicon Sampling - the process of using persona-prompted Large Language
Models (LLMs) to generate human-like data [1] - offers a promising way to
cut down monetary and time costs of research, specifically in areas where
a survey would typically be needed. A survey that would otherwise take
multiple months and thousands of dollars can be simulated in a lab in a
few hours for a trivial cost. If synthetic data can truly approximate real
responses, this would accelerate social science research, and could be used to
inform various political decisions. However, the question of whether silicon
data actually approximates real data is very much up for debate. Silicon
Sampling remains a controversial method, and many researchers doubt the
accuracy of the synthetic data
